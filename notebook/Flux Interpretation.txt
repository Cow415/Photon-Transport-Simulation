Flux: time-resolved measure of photon energy flow through space
    Flux ~ Photon energy crossing a unit area per unit time

    SImulating disrete photon with weight -> record number of photon passing through the voxel

Flux is a 4D data set of flux(x, y, z,) over time steps of the
    At time step = __ index, with locations of photons and how much energy is moving.

Flux vlaue represents amount of light energy passing through the voxel at given time:
    High value => Many photon reached loation or photons retains high weight(low absorption)
    Low value => Few photons reaching / photon scattering and absorbtion

    Treat as illumination map in the sample -> 3D light distribution, "where light exist"

Equation Factors: 
🔹 Absorption (μa)
Reduces photon weight
Causes flux to decay with depth
🔹 Scattering (μs)
Redirects photons
Spreads flux spatially
🔹 Anisotropy (g)
Controls forward vs random scattering
Higher g → deeper penetration before spreading

Media Variation: 
Case 1: Air (low scattering, low absorption)
Light travels straight
Flux is narrow and directional
Sharp beam-like pattern

Case 2: Gel / tissue (moderate scattering)
Light spreads out
Flux becomes diffuse
Wider, smoother distribution

Case 3: Cell embedded in gel
Refractive/optical mismatch
You’ll see:
Shadowing
Localized flux changes
Possibly enhanced or reduced intensity zones

* MCX outputs different concepts:

Flux → directional energy flow (this simulation)
Fluence → total accumulated energy regardless of direction

Flux = better for time-resolved transport
Fluence = better for energy deposition / imaging
